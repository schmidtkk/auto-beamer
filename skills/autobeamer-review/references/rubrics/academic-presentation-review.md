# Academic-Presentation Review Rubric

Review `academic-presentation` decks as time-bounded academic sharing artifacts.

## Critical Checks

- The audience, timing, and venue are explicit or inferable.
- The opening establishes why the audience should care.
- The deck keeps one clear narrative thread and avoids manuscript-like density.
- The references slide is second-to-last before Thank You.
- Backup slides after Thank You prepare for likely technical questions.
- Claims are supported by evidence, citations, or backup material.

## Failure Modes

- Too much background for the time budget.
- Proofs or derivations that cannot be delivered at live-talk pace.
- A closing that ends on "future work" without a memorable takeaway.
- Missing backup slides for assumptions, ablations, proofs, or comparisons.
