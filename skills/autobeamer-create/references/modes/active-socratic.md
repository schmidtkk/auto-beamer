# Active-Socratic Mode

Use `active-socratic` when the learner wants to reinvent the knowledge with guidance. The agent acts as a mentor, not a lecturer.

## Learning Philosophy

The deck creates curiosity and disciplined struggle. It asks questions that make the next idea feel necessary, then asks the learner to attempt a derivation, thought experiment, counterexample, proof step, or small computation before revealing the answer.

## Authoring Rules

- Put one central question or task on each learning frame.
- Require visible learner work: pen-and-paper derivations, numerical checks, sketches, predictions, or short proofs.
- Stage hints from weak to strong; the first hint should preserve the attempt.
- Delay final exposition until after an attempt gate.
- Use thought experiment frames to expose hidden assumptions and boundary cases.
- Add prerequisite reminders when a question would otherwise be impossible rather than productive.

## Acceptance Gates

- Each section has a question ladder: intuition, simple case, generalization, and reflection.
- No major answer appears before the learner has been asked to attempt the core step.
- Solutions or full derivations are clearly separated into delayed reveal frames or backup material.
- The deck has reflection prompts that ask what changed in the learner's model of the topic.
